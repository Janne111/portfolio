Digital Tools

The assignment:

Our assignment till christmas was to create a digital tool of our own. We were totally free in the execution and what kind of tool we wanted to create.

Preparation & sketches:

So before we got started with our tool, Katharina had us answer a few questions. This way we were supposed to collect ideas about what kind of ways a tool could be constructed. She sowed us different aspects to consider when creating a new project and made us think about who the tool is for, what it should provide and what we need to keep in mind during the process.
After some brainstorming we started with rough sketches of the website, its layout and elements we wanted to include.

Some examples I came up with were:

  • A statue png generator,
  • A tea tester,
  • A bug that spawns bugs over your page,
  • A tool to create your own tea blends and rate them

My tool:

During sketching i had the Idea for a Tea-Tester where you could create your personal tea blends and rate them. It was quite cute, sounded fun and fancy. SO goin with this idea, i went straight into setting up the website. A mistake. Dont do that.
In my first consultation, the stuff I had was basically just random, not very thought through ideas and snippets of code. There was no real concept and it was focussed on too many different things. Like it didn't have a clear purpose.
So we talked it through and determined, that it wasnt really the generator I wanteed to create. Rather I wanted to expand on the storytelling and specific atmophere of tea and a tea shop.
So I began with what I could actually do well: Drawing. Drawing a location and adding figures and elements to it. Katharina showed me a website with a similar functionality as the one I was going for and I used that for inspiration. I really felt like a pixel aesthetic would fit the website amazingly, so I went to my friend for recommendation and ended up with a Resprite subscription.
Because I drew all the visuals beforehand, I didnt really think a lot about the layout and UI elements of the website. It wasnt too much work assembling everything this way, because I could easily scale and position everything in the drawing programm. The challenge was rather to make the website interavtive and figuring out javascript functions to do so.

It was so fun to keep adding elements, seeing how they fit into the webstie and how they became interactive. I coul've really kept going, as there are a ton of ideas i could still add. It's really amazing how everything just keeps flowing, when you have found the right concept and have a clear direction. So no need to say i really enjoyed this project! Generally I feel like the website as a tool is so great, because it's so versatile and makes for so many different options. Also you can always come back, update and change things, which is super interesting process. (It's also never truly finished tho, you just kinda have to stop yourself...)

Even though I have'nt really learned all the basics and the "industry standard" for web and coding, I feel like this more personal and free approach was a great hooker for getting into webdesign. Im also very proud of what i made and how it turned out. It fits its purpose of being like a cozy place with some immersive storytelling. I can see myself continuing to work on it in the future.
