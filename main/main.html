Reflection

This semester has geniuenly been super interesting! We have worked with tools and concepts that I have personaly never worked with before. Therefore, it was very challenging for one, but also extremely exciting. Fancy, i might call it. I feel like I've discovered my liking for coding and digital design. This is definitely a field that I want to explore further in the next years of my studies.

For the way of learning, I think we had a pretty easy start. Not going straight into coding, but firstly understanding the concept of algorithms and the complexity of explaining something in a way understandable for a computer. Honestly im pretty grateful, because none of us really had an idea of how coding works.
On the other hand, We started on the tools with very little basic knowledge. While I understand that it would be difficult to teach everyone according to what direction their project will take, I feel like it would have been helpful, especially in the long run, to have a better understanding of the ground rules and of what you generally need to set up a good website/tool. But i must admit it was also kind of fun, going into a very personalized bigger project and learning by doing.

On the use of AI:

How much can you actually learn by using AI? Do I need to use AI? Should I?
Question that I am asking myself. Questions that are constantly asked around me too. I must admit, most times I'm just too lazy to use AI. Properly at least. I do not know which models are actually helpful and i don't feel like it's worth the efford. Especially with the negative effects on the environment, as well as the fact that I want to learn for myself. I want my work to be created by myself. This way finishing a project or even making progress fells like a real accomplishment.

On the other hand, working with Visual Studio Code has made me use the built in AI features and therefore led me to trying out implementing AI in my workflow. To be honest it is pretty cool and helpful, especially for simple suggestions and auto fill. This makes everything a lot faster! I do feel like the chatbot tells me a lot of bullshit though. Like half its answers are not working, and the other overcomplicates things, that have a pretty simple solution. I feel like I'd have to learn the basics by myself a lot more, before actually being able to use the AI propely. But then again, if i knew all the stuff myself, i wouldnt need it at all.
The only thing it really has helped me with, was giving me the terminology/ideas of what to search for, if i had a specific problem i couldnt find a solition for by googling. (Most questions/problems have been solved in some forum anyway, you just need to find it.)
