Our collection:

We were tasked to make our own collection over the holidays.
As a group, we went with sweets/snacks. We imagined you could get a lot of interesting shapes and colors from the many different snacks there are. (Also christmas is like the perfect time for getting a lot of snacks anyways.)

The project

One word: Frustrating.
Firstly everyone was sick all the time. First week no one was there (one person per day at most), second week we were two. Of three. So it was hard to get anything done, or to coordinate and make a concept together. We kinda missed all the introdustions and basics and really only started in the second week, which already made it a little stressful. We did decide on a concept that was pretty fun and not too much work.

We wanted to categorize the snacks we collected and personalize each group. This way we would write a horoscope like paragraph for each of them and archive that in our publication.
But first, we started out with experimenting with the snacks, scanning them, extracting colors and shapes and printing with them. We got some patterns and texture for later use and some interesting pictures. Especially the shape of this one piece of candy was pretty cool though, so we wanted to implement this in the project. It was so nice to work with, that we decided to keep it as a constant element and the main shape for backgrounds and color. Thats why you will find the s shape and the yellow/Pink color scheme in the whole publication.


For the front and back we decided on a Title page with a slogan that Hannes had designed from an assembly of his collection with letters made of snacks. the important info, such as our names and the description, were placed in a grid, made out of the candy-shape. It looks pretty cool, especially with the color combination recreating the gradient of the candy.
for the inside I designed the horoscopes on top of a background of layered scans of the shape. Working with the way of the riso printer i wanted to create contrast by layering the same shape in different transparencies. Neat.
The poster side was another or Hannes' designs, featuring everything of the fotos and textures we havent been able to use before.
For the inlet, we just wanted a simple design, showcasing the whole colection and each individual item.

Overall, the project was one of the most stressful ones, but I think we still managed to create something pretty cool. The printin was also pretty much a mess, but i guess thats art of the process. rial and error. I'm happy we ended up with a resentable publication and an interesting experience.
